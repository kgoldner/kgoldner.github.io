The Interdependent Ivory Tower

August 6, 2021

The interdependent value model first introduced by Milgrom and Weber in 1982 (and Nobel-prize-winning as of 2021!) represents the concept that (1) key information to one person, Alice, may be held by another person, Bob, and as a result, (2) Alice maybe not be able to determine what she wants and how to make choices as a result. For example, on the job market, an applicant Alice has information about her own preferences of schools, but she lacks information about the schools' other interviewees, or future hiring plans, which may make a school more or less attractive to her. Many of the key stages in academia are about understanding the processes, making contacts, and extracting the information that other people have in order to best figure out your own valuation despite the interdependence on others' private information (and actions)!

I'm starting this blog as a commitment mechanism to talk about some of the following topics that I've either been asked about, or that I believe don't have enough awareness and visibility:

  • my experience on the job market
  • reviewing and a typical load
  • the faculty perspective that might be useful to know as a student
  • time and to-do management
  • struggles from physical and mental conditions while in academia
  • forming interdisciplinary collaborations
and much more.
